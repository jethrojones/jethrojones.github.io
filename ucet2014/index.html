April 3 at 2:00 in Room 217

Disclaimer

Obviously, there is no way I can get through all of these in 60 50 minutes. That’s why I made this webpage! You can check these things out and take a little time learning more about them. I am going to go fast in my presentation, and I am going to cover a lot of ground. But I hope to whet your appetite with some of these tools that save me hours of time.

Also, I am a Mac geek, so many of these things will be focused on Mac and iOS. While I have used PCs (I used to build them) and Android, I don’t use them much now and I don’t have much context for them.

I am not sharing anything that I do not use in some way, nor am I sharing anything that does not help me manage my time and life and an administrator.

Productivity

  1. Read Quick (with Instapaper or Pocket) - Read web pages super fast. ReadQuick Demo Gif
  2. TextExpander - Long things you type can be typed by small snippets. For example, when I type wemail it expands to my work email address, jethro.jones@canyonsdistrict.org (Also available for iOS) TextExpander Demo Gif
  3. Skitch For iOS - For Mac - Annotate Images. Demo Gif
  4. Hazel - Automate processes on your mac. This app is amazing.
  5. Automator - Comes with every Mac. Want to resize photos in a folder without opening Photoshop or something like it? This workflow will do it.
    Bonus points for using Hazel to have that happen as soon as you put the file in the folder.
  6. Keyboard Maestro. Ever have to copy something from one program to another (School Land TRUST plans & Excel spreadsheets anyone?) ⌘+SHIFT+C will copy, switch to another application or window, paste it, move to next area to paste, then switch back to your original application or window. BAM! iPhone Control App if you want to get really nerdy.
  7. Paperless Principal - This is a book I wrote that explores how to be a paperless principal. I save countless hours each week using the strategies in this book.
  8. Basecamp, educators can get a free account. Project Management software for your school. This is for the $100/month package (100 projects, 40GB of space). That is some serious savings.
  9. Google Voice - New number that can record calls and provide text message logs. Very handy for texting parents. My number is (801) 7-JETHRO. Call me, maybe.
  10. 1Password. Mac and iOS - Greatest vault of passwords ever. Generates and saves passwords like “gimi8jo6rha7mib” then it will autofill them for you.
  11. Evernote - You really should know about this by now. ;) Virtual junk drawer.
  12. Sanebox - filters out email that doesn’t need to interrupt you (they sponsor my podcast).
  13. Launch Center Pro for iPhone. Create workflows that do things faster. Launch Center Pro Video
  14. SCOA app - Classroom observations with data collection built in. Ugly, but great walkthrough app.
  15. Mynd - different looking app for calendar management. Gives you an overview of your day, especially nice if you have pictures with your contacts.
  16. PopClip - This app’s menu appears when you select text with your mouse. It allows you to cut, copy, paste, search, link, lookup and more from the little contextual menu. (You can even add extras here)
  17. Soulver This app allows you to write out your math problems rather than just enter numbers in a calculator. For iOS and Mac

  18. BufferApp - For iPhone - Posts things to the social media later.

Blogs

  1. Connected Principals - I follow that blog, when a couple posts by the same person speak to me, I start following that person’s blog.
  2. Storytelling with Data - Cole Nussbaumer @storywithdata is really good about teaching you how to use data to tell a story. Tips and tricks for making it look great and communicate what you want it to communicate.
  3. 21st Century Principal - Principal who blogs about everything from politics to technology to whatever else. Usually thoughtful prose. Most important items bolded to make for a fast read.
  4. Practical Theory - Chris Lehmann, Principal of the Science Leadership Academy in Philadelphia blogs about the issues facing him. Powerful recent posts about the budget crisis in Philadelphia, but he also blogs about many other educational topics.
  5. Principal’s Page - Michael Smith is actually a superintendent, but still blogs sarcastically about educational topics. He also has a bunch of great stuff not on the blog.
  6. The Wejr Board - One of the best interviews on my podcast (coming soon) was with Chris Wejr @chriswejr. He is very thoughtful and he has a very different take on student rewards and discipline than most that I have spoken with in education.
  7. Ed Chats (More info here) - While not a blog exactly, ed chats give you the opportunity to discuss educational topics on the twitter. Use hashtags (#) to denote affiliation with something. (#cpchat, #edchat, #satchat) Pro tip: use official twitter client to search for chat, so you can start a new tweet from the search screen, with the chat hashtag already supplied.

Web Automation

    IFTTT - (And for iPhone) This web app allows you to do automated things on the Internet. "If [a certain event] occurs online, then [another event] will be triggered." These are called recipes, and they are amazing.

    1. Automatically send posts on web site to Twitter.
    2. Shipping Notification to Junecloud Shipping Notification Service.
    3. I use Foursquare to check in to places for mileage reimbursement. This creates a text file in Dropbox with all the places I have been listed. Then, I use Google maps to map out my mileage reimbursement.
    4. This recipe will do the same thing as the previous one, but it will allow me to have a spreadsheet format, which may work better. I haven’t used this to do mileage reimbursement, but it has been triggered 131 times, so the timing is probably right. ;)
    5. Note to self - Sends me an email with an mp3 and a transcript. Pretty awesome.
    6. Wish friends a happy birthday automatically. Never forget another birthday!
    7. Keep your Facebook and Twitter profile pics in sync.
    8. Need to end a meeting? Send a text, get a phone call, politely excuse yourself.
    9. When you upload to Facebook, save the photo to Dropbox
    10. Automatically Post Blogger post to Twitter (different than Buffer above).

General Tech Tips

  1. Managing Personal and School Twitter accounts (Use different apps for each account Tweetbot for personal, Twitterific for professional).
  2. Learn Mac shortcuts and Learn Pc Shortcuts
  3. Create your own keyboard shortcuts Mac or PC. My favorite one is ⌘+SHIFT+, to open System Preferences (it makes sense because ⌘+, opens preferences in most programs).
  4. Time out iPad. If you have a kid who may use the iPad for 5 minutes, you can simply set the timer and set the alarm notification to “Stop Playing” which will then lock the iOS device so the passcode must be entered again.
  5. Find my iPhone Set this up on all iOS devices that you use at your school, make sure you can locate them when you need to.
  6. Use a passcode on your Phone and other devices, there is too much valuable information stored there.
  7. Use an RSS Reader make web sites come to you instead of you going to them. I use Feedly.
  8. ⌘+Shift+[ or ] moves between tabs with the keyboard in Safari, Chrome, Firefox.
  9. Update your computer regularly.
  10. Backup your data often.
  11. Tinker
  12. Remember, technology exists to make our lives easier: ensure it does.
  13. Have fun bending technology to your whims.
  14. Don’t be afraid to play around and learn new things.
